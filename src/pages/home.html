Airway-Focused Orthodontics

An airway-focused approach considers the jaw, palate, and airway together, and treatment is designed to support healthy breathing and better sleep alongside a confident smile.

Questions We Hear a Lot

Plain answers are part of the job. If yours is not here, just ask at your visit.

When should my child first see an orthodontist? +−
A good rule of thumb is around age 7, when adult teeth start coming in. Most kids will not need treatment that early, and that is good news. An early evaluation simply lets Dr. Williams watch growth and time things well if care is needed later.
Braces or Invisalign, which is right for us? +−
It depends on the smile in front of us, and that is exactly what a free consult is for. Both can work beautifully. Dr. Williams explains what he sees in plain language and lays out the options, so the choice is yours.
What happens at the free consultation? +−
You meet Dr. Williams, he takes a friendly look at teeth, bite, and jaw growth, and you leave with clear options and honest timing. No pressure, no rushing, and nothing to pay for the visit.
Do you take our insurance? +−
Most insurance is accepted, and our team helps you understand your benefits before anything starts. Flexible monthly payment plans are available too.
What is airway-focused orthodontics? +−
It means looking at how the jaw, palate, and airway work together, not just at straight teeth. Treatment is designed to support healthy breathing and sleep alongside a confident smile. If you have noticed snoring or mouth breathing, mention it at your consult and we will take a look together.
Do we need a referral from our dentist? +−
No referral needed. You can request a free consult directly, and we are happy to coordinate with your family dentist along the way.
